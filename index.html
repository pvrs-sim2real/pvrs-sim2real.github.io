Large-Scale Study of Pre-Trained Visual Representations in Sim and Real Environments

Abstract

We conduct a large-scale empirical study about use of pre-trained visual representations (PVRs) to train policies for real-world robotics tasks. Our study spans five different PVRs, two different learning paradigms, and three different robots for manipulation and indoor navigation. Our study reveals three major insights: performance trends with PVRs in simulation are indicative of real-world trends; we achieve a first-of-its-kind result with indoor ImageNav; and we find data augmentation and fine-tuning to be generally beneficial, while the ideal model-size of PVR depends on the policy learning paradigm.
